Welcome to GetHelpLex

Alcohol and drug addiction affects individuals from all walks of life.  Locating treatment, support, or other resources for yourself or a loved one with addiction may seem overwhelming.

 Whether you are looking for services or support for yourself or a loved one, there are local, state, and national resources to help you locate treatment and recovery services as well as support for family and friends.

GetHelpLex is a tool to help you find resources related to alcohol and other drug addiction. It is an informational tool only.

Casey's Law

Casey's Law allows the parents, relatives, or friends of a person with addiction to lawfully intervene and request involuntary, court-ordered addiction treatment.

For more information on Casey’s Law, please visit the Kentucky Office of Drug Control Policy’s Casey Law page at the link below:
https://odcp.ky.gov/Stop-Overdoses/Pages/Caseys-Law.aspx.

For faith-based help with you or your loved one's addiction connect with SPARK Ministries at:
http://www.kyspark.org.

No-Cost Narcan/Naloxone In Lexington

No-cost Narcan/Naloxone is available at the Lexington-Fayette County Health Department located at 650 Newtown Pike in Lexington during the Syringe Service Program:

Mondays: 1:00 p.m. to 4:00 p.m.
Wednesdays: 3:00 p.m. to 6:30 p.m.
Fridays: 11:00 a.m. to 4:00 p.m.

To receive no-cost Narcan/Naloxone, the recipient must complete a brief training on how to administer it.

If you’d like to receive Narcan/Naloxone at an alternative time, please contact the Harm Reduction Program at the Lexington-Fayette County Health Department at call (859) 899-4230.

No-Cost Assessment In Lexington

Peace Assessment and Referral Center provides no-cost level of care assessments at many Lexington area hospitals, including Saint Joseph Hospital, Saint Joseph East, Saint Joseph Jessamine and Saint Joseph Berea.

Peace Assessment and Referral Center is located at 1401 Harrodsburg Road, Suite A-400 in Lexington.

The center is open Monday through Friday from 8:00 a.m. until 9:00 p.m.

To schedule an appointment, call 859-313-3515. Walk-ins are also welcome.

About GetHelpLex

GetHelpLex is a resource for people seeking facilities and services for substance use disorder (substance abuse/addiction) in and around Lexington, Kentucky. GetHelpLex is an open-source project. GHL is an adaptation of Code for Boston's Finda project. The source code for both Finda and GHL are publically available on GitHub.

Application creation and support from:

LFUCG Substance Abuse and Violence Intervention Program

The Substance Abuse and Violence Intervention (SAVI) Program works with community stakeholders and advocates in Lexington to increase programming and opportunities that reduce drug related violence by teens and young adults; decrease the stigma that inhibits recovery; advocate for and increase access to effective substance abuse treatment programming.

Voices of Hope – Lexington, Inc.

Voices of Hope – Lexington is a non-profit organization dedicated to creating a positive environment to help people addicted to drugs and alcohol enter and flourish in recovery.

Cogitools Software

Cogitools Software is a technology consultancy based in Lexington, KY.

OpenLexington

OpenLexington is a volunteer group of civic-minded software developers, designers, urban planners, citizen activists, data geeks and more. Part of the Code for America Brigade program, we work with LFUCG, Fayette County, and local communities to build open-source civic apps, open public data sources, and generally help to leverage technology for use in the public sphere.

LexLadiesCode

LexLadiesCode is a women's coding club based in Lexington, Kentucky gathered to increase the visibility of and possibilities for women in technology.

Code for Boston

Code for Boston aims to provide a 'center of gravity' for grassroots, community-driven civic innovation in the Greater Boston area. Our work includes building apps, community organizing, policy initiatives, qualitative research, and more, and we hope that volunteers of all types will join our community.
